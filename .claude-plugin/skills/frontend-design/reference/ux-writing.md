# UX Writing

## Core Principles

- **Make every word earn its place**
- **Plain language always wins** over jargon
- **Active voice** — "Save changes" not "Changes will be saved"
- **Short sentences** — cut every sentence in half, then do it again

## Labels

- Be specific, not clever — "Sign in" not "Unlock the experience"
- Use sentence case — "Sign in to your account" not "Sign In To Your Account"
- Actions should be verbs — "Save", "Delete", "Send invite"

## Error Messages

State what happened + what to do:

- **Bad**: "An error occurred"
- **Good**: "We couldn't save your changes. Check your internet connection and try again."

## Empty States

Teach the interface, not just "nothing here":

- **Bad**: "No tasks yet"
- **Good**: "You haven't created any tasks yet. Click + New Task to get started."

## Microcopy

- Button text should describe the action: "Delete project" not "Submit"
- Confirmation dialogs: "Delete 'Project Name'?" not "Are you sure?"
- Undo messages: "Draft deleted" not "Success"

**Avoid**: Repeating information users can already see. Technical jargon. Hedging language ("might", "could", "perhaps").
